It looks like you may not have a good data or cell connection to set up Google Allo. Here are some steps you can try:

What you need

  1. Wi-Fi or mobile data connection to send a phone verification request
  2. Cellular connection to receive the one-time SMS with the verification code

Check your connection

  • Check your settings to make sure Wi-Fi or mobile data is turned on and airplane mode is turned off.
  • Move to where you have a better mobile data connection. Your network signal icon should have at least 1 bar.
  • Test your connections by sending yourself an SMS or visiting a website.

When you're connected

  • After you have data and cell signals, you can ask for the verification code again by tapping Get a new code.
  • If you have a cell connection and aren't getting the SMS, request a call by tapping Call me.
  • To send and receive messages on Allo, you only need Wi-Fi or a mobile data connection.

Still having problems?

  • If the app is stuck: Quit Allo by going to your device's Settings > Apps > Allo > Force Stop. Open Allo again.
  • Uninstall and re-install the app: Try uninstalling Allo. Then re-download Allo and open the app.
  • Restart your phone: Try turning your phone off and turning it back on. Then open Allo again.
  • Find help or send feedback in the app: If you get connected to Wi-Fi or mobile data, tap the question icon.
